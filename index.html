Christian Calinawan

For this assignment, our group chose “Give way to your worst impulse” as our instruction. We interpreted this something along the lines of giving into a temptation, whatever that may be. For me, an everyday temptation is sleeping in longer than I should. I depicted this through illustrating myself in bed turned away from a window letting light into the room with also a clock showing that the time is well into the afternoon. I made this first thing after sleeping in then waking up.

Natalie Bellido

Our group chose to do the 'give way to your worst impulse' strategy. My worst impulse is to look at my phone so I chose to count how many times I did that over two days. Each time I picked up my phone, I made a note for why I did it and would edit the number for each time. At the end of the second day, I decided to make something to represent the results of this little experiment. This resulted in me drawing a phone with icons and numbers over them. The icons represent the reason I checked my phone and the numbers are how many times. I think that my piece turned out good, but I do wish I could have done more with it since it looks so simple. At first I thought about putting the icons on the canvas for the amount of times, but it seemed to be too much work so in the end I decided to simplify it. My project was made in a free drawing program called Krita and I used the shape and drawing tools to create it.
